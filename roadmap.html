🚀 The Complete Launch Roadmap

From Zero to a Global Astrology Consulting Business

Built directly on the verified research in this pack. Numbers from research pages are sourced facts; numbers in this plan marked plan are planning targets we set and will validate in the first 90 days — the two are never mixed.

The business, precisely defined

What we sell

  • Live 1:1 consultations (video/phone, recorded): natal chart, compatibility, career, year-ahead, timing questions — priced in the professional band the market verifies ($150–300 US / £130–150 UK / 140–150€ FR — see pricing evidence)
  • Written & recorded readings (async, no calendar limit): the margin products
  • Subscriptions (monthly/half-yearly/yearly) + a one-time 150-seat founding-member LTD
  • Phase-2: remedies & gemstone commerce attached to consultations, jewelry/tradition-framed (never health claims)

The two structural facts the whole plan respects

  • The partner is the product — and the bottleneck. One astrologer at 6 sessions/day, 5 days/week, $200 average = ~$26K/month gross ceiling on live work alone plan. Everything above that ceiling must come from async products, subscriptions, group formats, and eventually additional vetted astrologers (the AstroTalk path).
  • The paying core is the 6–10%, not the 30%. Per Pew (verified), the serious-guidance segment is ~16–26M US adults. We sell premium consulting to a identifiable niche — women 18–49, millennials, LGBT adults, plus the Vedic/diaspora lane — not horoscope entertainment to everyone.

Phase 0 — Foundations (weeks 1–4)

Entity & money

  • Entity: a US LLC (Delaware or Wyoming, via Stripe Atlas) is the standard playbook when the operating partner is outside the US — it unlocks US Stripe, US banking (Mercury), and buyer trust in the primary market. If the astrologer partner is UK-resident, a UK Ltd does the same job. Decision input needed: partner's country of residence and tax situation — this is the one blocking question for week 1.
  • Payments architecture (non-negotiable, from verified research): Stripe as primary (subscriptions + checkout) — astrology is NOT on Stripe's US/CA/UK/FR/EU restricted lists, but the vertical is de-facto high-risk with documented discretionary closures. So: clean website language at underwriting time, low-dispute billing design, AND a signed-up backup high-risk merchant account from day one (CorePay and PayKings are the documented psychic/tarot-vertical providers from the research sources), with checkout able to fail over. Keep 4–6 weeks of revenue as float against fund-hold risk.
  • Partnership agreement before any money moves (the 50% stake): brand/IP and client-list owned by the company, not either person; vesting or buy-back terms; and the key-person clause — the business's #1 risk is the astrologer walking away with the audience. Also fix: who owns the ad accounts, the domain, the Stripe account.

Legal pack (every item verified on Rules)

  • Site legal: own Terms, Privacy Policy, Refund/Cancellation policy, Disclosure — hosted on our domain. Disclaimer: "guidance — not a substitute for professional medical, legal or financial advice" + 18+ age gate (the Keen-standard formulation; "entertainment only" is not legally required and undercuts a premium brand).
  • EU/UK consent architecture (CNIL Cosmospace is the template): explicit sensitive-data consent checkbox in the booking flow ("readings may touch relationships, wellbeing and beliefs — I explicitly consent to this data being processed"); no blanket call recording (client-requested recordings only); ≤3-year marketing data retention; consent logs.
  • Withdrawal-right mechanics (EU/UK/FR): "start now + I acknowledge losing my 14-day withdrawal right on full performance" checkbox before any reading is delivered inside 14 days.
  • When EU sales begin: appoint an Art. 27 EU representative (and UK rep) — subscription services exist for this (~€50–150/mo plan). Cookie consent banner per CNIL/ICO. France spend-cap policy: automatic review + cooling-off outreach for repeat high-spend customers (Art. 223-15-2 defense-by-design).
  • Email compliance set to the strictest law we touch (CASL): logged express consent, sender postal address in footer, unsubscribe honoured immediately. No purchased lists ever.

Brand

  • One brand, new name, own domain (.com), designed to carry both Western and Vedic service lines. The astrologer's certifications, years of practice, and existing UK/FR client base are the EEAT backbone — real bio, real credentials (every factual claim documented BEFORE publishing: CAP 3.7 rule), face on the site, client testimonials collected with written permission and no outcome claims.

The compliant-copy card (printed from the verified Rules)

Every ad may

  • Describe the service, duration, price, credentials (documented), format
  • Speak third-person: "readings for people navigating career changes"
  • Use social proof without outcome claims ("500+ charts read" — if documented)
  • Offer the transparent $95 Discovery intro (real price, shown)

No ad, page or email ever

  • Promises outcomes: reunions, wealth, health, luck, "guaranteed accuracy"
  • Second-person distress copy: "anxious about your marriage?" (Meta ban)
  • Health/wealth/love improvement claims (UK CAP), gambling/lottery help (EU per-se ban)
  • "Free reading" hooks that convert to billing (the FTC Miss Cleo trigger)

Tracking & 90-day validation targets plan

  • GA4 + server-side conversion tracking; call/booking tracking through Cal.com webhooks; per-channel MER dashboard from week 1.
  • Validation gates at day 90: blended CPA for a paid booking ≤ 40% of first-order value (≤ ~$80 on a $195 session); ≥25% of month-1 clients booking a follow-up or subscribing within 60 days; refund rate ≤5%; dispute rate ≤0.4% (processor safety). Miss two gates → fix funnel before scaling spend. AstroTalk's verified benchmark (CAC $6–9, 80% repeat revenue) is India-blended and NOT our target — ours are set from our own price points.

The Founders' Circle LTD (Dominic's 100–200-seat idea, structured to what the niche's economics verify)

The verified constraint: in-niche and deal-platform precedent prices lifetime CONTENT cheap — Moonly's $39.99 lifetime sits at or below its own annual price, and AppSumo LTDs average $69–99. No precedent supports lifetime unlimited human service — that's an unbounded liability on the one astrologer's calendar. So the LTD sells lifetime content + a fixed, finite service allocation:
  • Founders' Circle — 150 seats, $999 one-time plan: lifetime Stargazer content (the ≈$414 content component after session value ≈ 2.3× its annual price — deliberately above app-content norms because it's personalized, not canned) + 3 live 45-min sessions usable in the first 24 months (~$585 catalog value, finite) + permanent 20% off all future sessions & store + founding-member badge and first-refusal on new offerings.
  • Why it works: 150 × $999 = $149,850 plan of launch capital against a bounded 450-session obligation (~19 sessions/month over 24 months — schedulable), while seeding 150 lifetime advocates. Sold in two waves (50 founding / 100 early) to create honest scarcity — seat counter shown, real.

Phase 4 — The gemstone & remedies arm (months 6–12)

Built only after consultation volume exists, because the verified model (AstroTalk Store: ₹0 → ₹200Cr ARR in ~14 months) attaches commerce to trusted advice — standalone Western crystal retail verifies at just $2–5M/yr for the biggest brands (see Expansion).

  • Sequence: (1) months 6–8: curated dropship/3PL of certified gemstones + malas/bracelets recommended in Vedic consultations, order links delivered in the post-session summary; (2) months 9–12: own inventory for the top-10 SKUs once demand data exists; own Shopify store on a subdomain.
  • Certification non-negotiable: every stone ships with a third-party lab certificate (IGI/GIA-grade, the GemPundit-standard practice) — certificates attest identity and treatment, and our product pages say exactly that.
  • Claims rule (from ASA/FTC verified record): sold as jewelry + Vedic tradition ("recommended for your chart per Jyotish tradition") — never health, wealth or healing claims. UK ads for the store follow the crystal-therapy precedents: zero therapeutic language.
  • Pricing: keystone-to-DTC convention (2–4× landed cost) on certified stones; AOV evidence from the niche: $50–75 (crystal e-com) for entry items, certified Vedic stones $150–800+ plan.
  • Payments: physical goods run on the same Stripe + backup rail; gemstone line kept on a separate sub-brand/merchant descriptor so a dispute spike there never threatens the consulting rail.
