The verdict in five lines

  • It's legal and advertisable everywhere we want to operate. Astrology consulting is not banned on Google, Meta, Microsoft or Stripe in US/CA/UK/FR/EU — France abolished its fortune-telling offence in 1994, Canada repealed its in 2018, US bans are mostly unconstitutional. Compliance is a copywriting and billing-design discipline, not a legal barrier.
  • The market is real but the paying core is 3–5× smaller than the hype. ~78M US adults touch astrology yearly, but the serious-guidance segment that pays is 6–10% of adults. The buyer is precisely identifiable: women 18–49, millennials, LGBT adults, plus the Vedic/diaspora lane.
  • The verified money models are per-minute/per-session human consultation and web subscriptions — AstroTalk hit ₹1,176 Cr (~$140M) FY25 revenue and a $300M valuation on consultations + commerce; OBRIO's Nebula runs ~$50M ARR. Pure content apps monetize far worse.
  • Every company that got hurt got hurt the same way: fake-free funnels, outcome promises, billing tricks. FTC's Miss Cleo: ~$500M forgiven (2002); CNIL's Cosmospace: €250K GDPR fine (2024); and in June 2026 the FTC sued Nebula's parent enterprise, with a federal court temporarily halting parts of its US operations. All three are design choices we simply don't make.
  • The plan works within one hard constraint: the astrologer partner is both the product and the bottleneck (~$26K/mo solo live-session ceiling) — so the roadmap builds async products, subscriptions, a bounded 150-seat LTD, and a consultation-attached gemstone arm around him.
