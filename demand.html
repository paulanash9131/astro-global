✦ Deliverable 3 · What People Want From Astrology

The Actual Demand: Questions, Formats, Retention

Customers don't buy "astrology" — they buy answers to four questions: love, career, money, timing. Below is what the evidence shows about what they ask, how they want it delivered, and what makes them come back.

Why people come — the motivation split

Pew (verified 3-0): of the 30% of US adults who engage at least yearly, 20 points do it "just for fun" and only 10 points for "helpful insights". Both groups buy — but they buy different products.

🎡 The fun-seekers (20% of adults)

  • Buy: horoscope content, compatibility novelty, gift readings, low-ticket reports
  • Behavior: impulse purchases, social sharing, low retention
  • Role for us: cheap top-of-funnel — content audience and entry-offer buyers who feed the email list

🧭 The guidance-seekers (10% of adults, 6% weekly)

  • Buy: 1:1 consultations, follow-up sessions, ongoing memberships
  • Behavior: return at every life decision — job change, breakup, relocation, marriage, investment
  • Role for us: the entire subscription and high-ticket business lives here

Source: Pew Research, May 2025 (verified 3-0)

Career is a first-class product category, not a love-reading afterthought.

Platform evidence — top question categories users bring to astrologers (Astroyogi, major consultation platform; unquantified platform-reported ranking)

❤️ Love & marriage — two separate categories

Compatibility ("is my partner right for me?") and marriage timing ("when will I meet someone / what age will I marry?") are distinct top categories — timing questions are their own product.

💼 Career & business

"What type of job should I seek?", "should I start my own business?", government vs private employment. Maps directly to a career-consultation SKU.

💰 Money & investment timing

"When should I invest?" is a distinct top use-case. NOTE: this is also the highest-compliance-risk category — see Rules — answered as timing guidance, never as financial advice.

Vedic vs Western demand differs: the platform's Vedic (Indian) question set includes categories absent from Western demand — auspicious baby-name letters, foreign-travel prospects, property/inheritance timing. A Vedic-certified astrologer serving the Indian diaspora in US/UK/CA sells a different question menu than a Western-astrology brand — the service catalog must carry both.

Sources: Fortune, Aug 2024 (EduBirdie survey) · Astroyogi top-questions — both attributed, not independently verified surveys

Retention mechanics the incumbents use

  • Discounted intro offer. Keen's current offer: 10 minutes for $1.99 (earlier variants: 5-for-$1, 3 free minutes). The industry-standard loss-leader — legal when the billing after the intro is transparent (this exact funnel done deceptively is what killed Miss Cleo — see Rules).
  • The natal chart as gateway. Documented practitioner mechanic: first birth-chart reading discounted (storefront psychics: ~$10–20) because the natal chart is the foundation every future session builds on — transits, returns, compatibility all reference it. First reading = onboarding, not profit.
  • Life-event re-triggering. Guidance-seekers return at decision moments (new job, breakup, move, investment). Email/SMS sequences keyed to astrological events (retrogrades, eclipses, the client's solar return = birthday) give a compliant, natural re-contact calendar.
  • The astrologer relationship IS the retention. Per-minute marketplaces run advisor favorites/follow features; independents run recurring check-ins. Clients stay with the astrologer who knows their chart — which is why our certified-partner model (one trusted expert + team) retains better than anonymous marketplace rotation.
